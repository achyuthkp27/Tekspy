The Challenges of Remote Work

Nowadays, many companies are providing remote work to continue business operations during the pandemic. While working from home has its benefits, it also presents new challenges for employers, as it can be difficult to verify the authenticity of job seekers and employees.

This was the case for a reputed organization that received one employee who had recently joined the company. The team leader noticed that the employee was busy with some other calls, not involving work much, and take more than the targeted time to complete tasks with excuses, all of these things lead the company to suspect that something was amiss.

The company wants to dig deeper into his identity for verifying the employee in all possible ways, for that, they believe in our company efficiently and approach us. First, we verified his address and previous employment. We got to know that the employee was working for several other companies some of the companies offered permanent work-from-home.

Further investigation revealed that the employee was running a software training institute on the other side. That Employee assigned their work to students for practical experience and even passed off these projects as their own.

This case highlights the difficulties that can arise when it comes to managing remote employees. While virtual interviews and work-from-home arrangements can make it easier for them to do these frauds and manage work with someone and also hide real authentication of them to provide fake employment documentation so that it's harder to identify unethical or off-target employees. Employers must be vigilant in verifying the credentials of job candidates and keep an eye on the performance of their remote workers.

IMPORTANCE OF EMPLOYMENT VERIFICATION

As part of Every company hiring process to recruit a person, Company needs to check their previous employment through a streamlined verification team. For that purpose recently one small-scale company believes us and tie up for background verification of employees. They initiate employment verification for the candidate in question. However, when we tried to reach out to the candidate's previous company, we discovered that they were no longer physically located at the address listed on their previous payslip of the candidate. Despite this, we communicated with the company through emails, and calls and they promptly provided us with the necessary information to verify the candidate's employment.

Upon further investigation, we learned that this same candidate had been terminated from one of our client organizations just a few months prior. This raised red flags for us and we decided to further scrutinize the candidate's application and background.

After careful consideration, we ultimately decided not to move forward with the candidate's application. While they may have had the skills and experience required for the role, their lack of honesty and transparency in their job application was a concern for us.

In the end, it's important to check previous employment thoroughly for all job candidates, not just for the sake of the company, but also for the sake of your current employees and clients. By conducting thorough background checks and verification processes, you can ensure that you are bringing on board individuals who are honest and reliable, ultimately leading to a more positive and productive work environment for everyone.
