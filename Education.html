The Importance of Document Verification in Education History Check

Education Check

One aspect of the education verification process involves checking for any gaps in a candidate's education history. This can be important for companies to know, as education gaps may raise questions about a candidate's work history and commitment to their studies.

Another aspect of the education verification process involves checking for fake documents. Some candidates may provide forged or altered documents to misrepresent their educational history. To Avoid those aspects, Tekspy arises with expertise and resources to detect fake documents and discrepancies in a candidate's education history

Here are some steps that take to verify an employee's education:

Contact the institution

We communicate with the educational institution listed on the employee's resume or job application to confirm that the employee attended the institution and received the degree they claim to have earned.

Check public records

we may search public records to see if there is any information available about the employee's educational history like attendance, percentages, backlogs, and education gaps throughout the academic year. This could include searching online databases or contacting the educational institution directly.

Ask for documentation

we may ask the employee to provide documentation to support their educational claims. This could include transcripts, diplomas, or other official documents.

Verify the employee's employment history

we may also verify the employee's employment history to ensure that the information they provided is accurate and to see if the employee has relevant work experience for the position they are applying for.

Prepare a report

Once we gathered and verified all the necessary information, they will prepare a report for the employer outlining the employee's educational history and any other relevant information. This report can help the employer make an informed decision about whether to hire the employee

Tekspy is a background verification service that helps employers verify the accuracy of information provided on job applicants' resumes and in their previous employment history.
